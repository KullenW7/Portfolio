Text And Color

Text and color accessibility practices ensure that digital content is readable and inclusive for all users, including those with visual impairments. High contrast between text and background improves readability, with a minimum contrast ratio of 4.5:1 for normal text and 3:1 for large text recommended by WCAG. Avoid relying solely on color to convey meaning; instead, use text labels or icons. Sans-serif fonts enhance legibility, and sufficient spacing between lines and letters prevents strain. Providing options for users to adjust text size and color settings improves accessibility. Lastly, testing with screen readers and accessibility tools ensures a more inclusive experience.
